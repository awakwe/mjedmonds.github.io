Publication Date: December 16, 2011 | ISBN-10: 076378060X | ISBN-13: 978-0763780609 | Edition: 1. This is a terrific book. I have adopted it as the required textbook for CS 81 starting in fall 2013. Information on this book, as well as sample code, errata, additional exercises, student work samples, and bonus material is available at http://javascript.cs.lmu.edu/.

Recommended The JavaScript Programming Language by Ray Toal. Paperback: 104 pages; Publisher: Jones and Bartlett Publishers; 1 edition; (January 8, 2009); ISBN-10: 0763766585; ISBN-13: 978-0763766580. Dr. Toal, my professor at Loyola Marymount University, is the best computer science teacher I have ever known. I just finished this book and judge it valuable.

Recommended JavaScript: The Good Parts Unearthing the Excellence in JavaScript by Douglas Crockford; Publisher: O'Reilly Media; Released: May 2008; Pages: 176; ISBN: 978-0-596-51774-8. This is one of the best technical books I've ever read and is a must-read for anyone who wants to write excellent JavaScript.
